Aftermath

The Korean War ended in a stalemate with the signing of the Korean Armistice Agreement on July 27, 1953. The conflict was one of the most destructive of the modern era, resulting in millions of military and civilian deaths and leaving the Korean peninsula physically and economically devastated.

The war solidified the political division of Korea, creating the heavily fortified Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) near the 38th parallel that continues to separate North and South Korea today. No formal peace treaty was ever signed, meaning the two Koreas are technically still at war.

The conflict had a major impact on the Cold War, prompting increased military spending and solidifying the United States' policy of containment against communism.
